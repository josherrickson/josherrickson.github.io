Currently, I am a 6th year Statistics PhD candidate at the University of Michigan. I work with my advisor, Professor Ben Hansen, on research centered around causal inference in comparative studies, particularly observational studies in the social sciences.

I also work at CSCAR, providing one-on-one consultation for university researchers from a wide range of disciplines.

Previously, I have obtained a BS degree in Computer Science from Johns Hopkins University and a MS degree in Statistics from Rutgers, The State University of New Jersey.
